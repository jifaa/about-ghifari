# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary audience**: Cosplay community members — fellow cosplayers, event organizers, photographers, and fans who discover Ghifari through conventions, social media, or word-of-mouth.

**Secondary audience**: Potential employers, collaborators, or clients who appreciate the programmer + creative dual identity and want to see range and versatility.

The cosplay audience is primary: they discover the site, engage first, and form the initial impression.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that showcases Ghifari's dual creative identity — programmer by week, cosplayer by weekend — in a way that feels authentic, warm, and memorable. The site should make visitors think "this person is genuinely passionate about both" and want to follow along or collaborate.

**What success looks like**: Visitors understand the unique blend of technical and creative work, feel the personality behind both pursuits, and follow on Instagram or reach out.

## Positioning

Where code meets costume — a portfolio that proves the same person who builds web apps also brings fictional characters to life.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA**: Follow on Instagram (@thisghifari._/)
- **Secondary CTA**: View all projects / View all cosplay
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds**: "Programmer by day, cosplayer by weekend — and equally passionate about both."
- **Belief ladder**:
  1. This person takes both pursuits seriously (quality work, real projects)
  2. They're versatile and creative, not just technical
  3. Their work is worth following / they'd be interesting to collaborate with
- **Proof on hand**: Portfolio photos, cosplay gallery with photographer credits, live project links

## Brand Personality

**Professional but friendly** — the duality is the personality. Warm enough to feel personal, polished enough to take seriously. Like meeting a skilled craftsperson who also has a great sense of humor.

## Anti-references

- Template or AI-generated looking sites (generic layouts, obvious stock patterns)
- Cold or corporate-feeling portfolios
- Dark/neon developer portfolios that feel intimidating
- Any surface that makes the two identities feel disconnected rather than complementary

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell** — Let the work speak; the personality emerges from what's presented, not from labels or claims.
2. **Dual identity as strength** — Never apologize for or explain away the combination; treat it as a feature.
3. **Warmth with craft** — Every detail should feel considered but never sterile.
4. **Stand out, not just fit in** — Creative and unique beats safe and polished.
5. **Consistent across both pursuits** — The programming projects and cosplay work should feel like they belong to the same person.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance minimum
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — motion should enhance, not distract
- Color combinations must maintain contrast across the palette
- Semantic HTML structure for screen readers
